Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about land use planning in British Columbia

What is modernized land use planning?

Land use planning provides the vision, goals and objectives to guide sustainable resource stewardship and management of provincial public land. Modernizing land use planning is an approach led by the B.C. government in partnership with Indigenous governments and with engagement of communities, local government, industry, and other stakeholders.

What are land use plans used for?

Completed land use plans provide direction to support decisions about how the land is best used. Land use plans help to maintain environmental and cultural values and create economic opportunities through increased certainty.

Why is the Province modernizing land use planning?

The B.C. government is committed to collaborating with Indigenous governments in natural resource management that is informed by the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples and the Truth and Reconciliation Commission of Canada’s Calls to Action. The B.C. government is also committed to ensuring communities and stakeholders are engaged in land and resource planning throughout the province. Modernizing land use planning is an opportunity to fulfill these commitments while strengthening B.C.’s growing economy and adapting our resource management to the effects of climate change.

What is the Province’s approach to modernizing land use planning?

Both the B.C. government and Indigenous government(s) need to mutually agree to initiate a land use planning process. Each process will be designed and delivered in partnership with Indigenous governments and will include extensive engagement with the public and stakeholders to ensure all regional interests are identified. Unlike the large planning tables of past land use planning in B.C., modernized land use planning engagement will be targeted to make the most efficient use of time and resources.

Where will land use planning occur?

Land use planning will focus on priority areas to resolve current land and resource management challenges on provincial public lands and marine areas. It will not include federal lands and water, private lands, or agricultural land reserves.

An initial set of selected land use planning projects will begin in 2019-20. Additional priority projects, identified through discussions between the B.C. government, Indigenous governments, community groups and industry, will be evaluated and considered in future years.

How will Indigenous governments’ land use plans be considered in modernized land use planning?

Indigenous people in B.C. have been stewards of the land for thousands of years and possess extensive knowledge of forests, lands, waters, and ecosystems. As such, many Indigenous governments have developed land use plans for their traditional territories.

Modernizing land use planning requires the B.C. government and Indigenous government(s) to jointly develop land use plans. Entering into a government-to-government land use planning process means both governments are willing to work towards a single plan that has support from both governments.
