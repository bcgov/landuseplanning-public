Land Use Planning Project Phases

Land use planning projects in British Columbia will vary in complexity, scope, and timelines. However, there are common steps that are required to complete a land use plan, and have it approved and implemented.

Steps for Completing a Land Use Plan

  1. Pre-Planning. Preliminary discussions with First Nation(s) and key regional stakeholders on land use and management issues. Agree in principle with First Nation(s) to undertake a land use planning process, develop a business case and seek mandates to proceed.
  2. Plan Initiation. Formalize agreement with First Nation(s) to proceed with land use planning project, finalize planning scope, including a governance structure to oversee the process and provide support at key project milestones, develop a project budget, workplan and stakeholder engagement strategy.
  3. Plan Development. Apply information and planning tools to produce scenario analyses that address planning issues. Select a preferred planning scenario and prepare a draft plan.
  4. Plan Evaluation and Approval. Evaluate draft land use plan, provide dispute resolution if necessary and revise plan as required. Submit land use plan for review and approval by planning partners.
  5. Plan Implementation and Monitoring. Develop an implementation plan that includes associated agreements with planning partners and a secured implementation committee and budget.
