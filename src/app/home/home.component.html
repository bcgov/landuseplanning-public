Land Use Planning

Land use planning is a collaborative process of defining vision, goals and objectives to guide sustainable resource management of provincial public land in British Columbia. It is a key component of trusted resource stewardship that provides social, economic, cultural, and environmental benefits to citizens of British Columbia.

About this Site

This site is a central hub for all active land use planning projects in B.C. that aims to increase the transparency and clarity of natural resource planning in the Province. This site allows users to access project information and supporting documents, reports and analyses, and submit comments and feedback to project planning teams of active land use planning projects.

Provincial land use planning has a long history in B.C. The majority of provincial land use plans were completed throughout the 1990s and early 2000s. A library of all completed provincial land use plans can be accessed on the B.C. government’s Land Use Plans webpage.

Land use planning sets higher-level strategic direction to guide sustainable resource stewardship and management of provincial public land and waters. Access to other provincial stewardship initiatives including the Collaborative Stewardship Framework, the Environmental Stewardship Initiative, and Landscape Level Forestry Planning are not available on this site.
